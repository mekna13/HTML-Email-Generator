DON’T TENSE UP ABOUT VERB TENSES: CHOOSING THE RIGHT TENSE TO COMMUNICATE EFFECTIVELY (ENGLISH LANGUAGE PROFICIENCY)
Tuesday, June 3, 2025, Zoom
Time: 10:30am - 11:30am CDT
Facilitators: Michele Vick, Center for Teaching Excellence
Description: Grammatical errors can hinder successful spoken communication. Speakers can communicate more effectively when they utilize the appropriate verb tense. In this workshop, attendees will review and practice narrative verb tenses while discussing research and reviewing previously covered classroom topics. They’ll explore the pedagogical impact of reviewing prior learning. An optional assignment for extended practice will be available on Canvas. Learning Outcomes: Participants will be able to… 1. Utilize narrative tenses to recall previous lecture material and discuss connections to current content. 2. Produce narrative verb tenses effectively in the discussion of research findings, choosing the appropriate tense to convey temporal relationships. 3. Facilitate review activities during lab sessions or lectures, utilizing appropriate narrative tenses to reiterate and reinforce key concepts covered in previous classes.

SUGGESTION OR REQUIREMENT? USE THE RIGHT MODAL VERB TO GIVE CLEAR INSTRUCTIONS (ENGLISH LANGUAGE PROFICIENCY)
Tuesday, June 10, 2025, Zoom
Time: 10:30am - 11:30am CDT
Facilitators: Michele Vick, Center for Teaching Excellence
Description: Grammatical errors can hinder successful spoken communication. Speakers can communicate more effectively when they utilize the appropriate modal verbs (such as should, might, must) to convey obligations and suggestions. In this workshop, attendees will review and practice modal verbs in the context of giving instructions and corrective feedback. An optional assignment for extended practice will be available on Canvas. Learning Outcomes: Participants will be able to… 1. Review different modal verbs (such as should and have to) and compare their functions. 2. Demonstrate ability to use appropriate modal verbs to give instructions, conveying specific meaning and fostering clarity and understanding in the lab setting. 3. Discuss best practices for giving corrective feedback. 4. Employ modal verbs in the feedback process, showcasing the ability to convey constructive criticism and suggestions using appropriate modals.
